## Version A. Ready-to-send English cover letter

Dear Editor,

We are pleased to submit our manuscript entitled

**“A Physics-Interpretable Gated-Imaging Simulation and Anti-Shortcut Validation Framework for 3D Target and Planar False-Target Discrimination”**

for consideration for publication in *Optics & Laser Technology*.

Laser range-gated imaging can provide range-dependent observations beyond a single two-dimensional intensity image, and is therefore potentially useful for discriminating true three-dimensional targets from planar false targets. However, classifiers trained on simulated gated images may achieve high accuracy by exploiting non-physical shortcuts, such as absolute brightness differences, black frames, or rendering artifacts. As a result, accuracy alone is not sufficient evidence that a model has used the intended multi-gate physical cue.

This manuscript addresses that validation gap. We present a physics-interpretable Blender-based gated-imaging simulation and anti-shortcut validation framework for true 3D target versus planar false-target discrimination. The main contributions are:

1. a gated-imaging simulation pipeline in which true 3D targets produce depth-dependent three-gate responses, while planar false targets are generated by camera-depth flattening and rectangular pulse-gate overlap modulation;
2. an anti-shortcut validation protocol that includes scalar shortcut diagnosis, per-gate maximum normalization, grouped multi-view validation, and single-gate ablations;
3. a structured-nuisance failure-boundary analysis showing that clean/noisy training is insufficient, together with domain-randomized training that improves robustness under reflectance, background, and occlusion shifts.

On a four-view grouped validation protocol, the full three-gate stack outperforms single-gate baselines. After domain-mixed training, the mainline model reaches mean accuracies of 0.9394 / 0.9242 / 0.9091 / 0.9394 / 0.7727 under clean, light-noise, strong-noise, mild-nuisance, and strong-nuisance evaluation, respectively. We deliberately position the work as a simulation and validation framework for range-gated optical imaging, rather than as a new neural architecture or a deployment-ready recognition system.

We believe the manuscript is a good fit for *Optics & Laser Technology* because it connects laser range-gated imaging physics with a practical machine-learning validation problem in optical target discrimination. The study should be of interest to readers working on range-gated imaging, active optical sensing, simulation-based optical system evaluation, and robust target recognition.

We confirm that this manuscript is original, has not been published previously, and is not under consideration by another journal. All authors have approved the submission.
